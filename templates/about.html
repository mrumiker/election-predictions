About the Site


What is this?

This project compiles and analyzes data from two popular US election prediction websites: Predictit.org and Fivethirtyeight.com.

These two websites make predictions about elections using different methods:
Predictit is a betting website where users can make wagers about many different outcomes and scenarios in politics.
Fivethirtyeight uses statistical models of polls to make election predictions.

Why did you make this?

I was interested in comparing the results of these two websites and providing analysis to anyone else who was interested on the internet.

How did you make this?

I used a programming language called Python to access and analyze the data from the two sites.

How often do the numbers update?

Fivethirtyeight updates its data once a day.
PredictIt data is constantly updated.

What info can I get from this site?

Users can see the PredictIt markets with current PredictIt price, Fivethirtyeight Probability, the difference between the Predictit and Fivethirtyeight prediction, and a "Recommendation."
The Recommendation says "buy YES" if the Difference is 8 or more; "buy NO" if the Difference is -8 or less.

There is a BEST BETS menu that compiles all the "buy YES" and "buy NO" markets.

What is the purpose of all this?

As someone who checks both of these websites quite often, I am interested in the results based on "gut feeling" vs. statistical analysis, and I hope that the project gives some insight into this.
I don't actually make bets on PredictIt, and the intention is not to influence markets or offer real betting advice.
The "Recommendations" are for educational purposes and to give an easy way to interpret the results. How much credence you give these results is entirely up to you.
